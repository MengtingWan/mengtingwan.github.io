I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science at University of California, San Diego (UCSD), working with Prof. Julian McAuley. Before joining in UCSD, I received my master degree in Statistics from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2015, and my bachelor degree in Statistics from Peking University in 2013. I was also a student from the liberal arts college program (Yuanpei Program) of Peking University.

My research is on the intersection of data mining, machine learning and computational social sciences. I'm broadly interested in
  • developing computational approaches to model human opinions and behavior from massive real-world datasets;
  • analyzing large-scale human activity datasets, and discovering behavioral knowledge behind the data.
My research is supported by 2017-2019 Microsoft PhD Fellowship.
