I'm a PhD candidate from the Artificial Intelligence Group at University of California, San Diego (UCSD), working with Prof. Julian McAuley. Before joining in UCSD, I received my master's degree in Statistics from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, and my undergraduate degree in Statistics from Peking University. I was also a student from the liberal arts college program (Yuanpei Program) at Peking University.

My research is on the intersection of data mining, machine learning and computational social sciences. I'm broadly interested in
  • developing computational approaches to model human opinions and behavior from massive real-world datasets;
  • analyzing large-scale human activity datasets, and discovering behavioral knowledge.
My research was supported by 2017-2019 Microsoft PhD Fellowship.
