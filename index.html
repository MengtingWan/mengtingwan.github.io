I am a Senior Research Scientist at Microsoft's Office of Applied Research. Before Microsoft I was a scientist at Airbnb working on ML and NLP problems.

I obtained my Ph.D. at University of California, San Diego (UCSD), advised by Prof. Julian McAuley. Before that, I received my master's degree in Statistics from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, and my undergraduate degree in Statistics from Peking University. I was also a student from the liberal arts college program (Yuanpei Program) at Peking University.

My current research focuses on User-Centric Large Language Models (LLMs) and LLM-Based Human-AI Interactive Systems.

I remain interested in Recommender Systems, Graph Machine Learning, Computational Social Sciences, as well as re-thinking research problems in these areas in the context of LLMs.
